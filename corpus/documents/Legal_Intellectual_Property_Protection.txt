# Intellectual Property Protection Policy

The Meridian Corp Intellectual Property Protection Policy safeguards all proprietary innovations, trade secrets, and creative works generated by employees, contractors, and collaborators. Under the EHRC cycle IP framework, all intellectual property created during employment or using TRITON system resources is automatically assigned to Meridian Corp. Employees must execute the MOR-2023-007 invention disclosure agreement within 14 days of their hire date.

Patent applications are managed through the ASCEND IP module, with the Legal team conducting prior art searches within the standard 14-day SLA. The NEXUS system tracks all pending applications, granted patents, and trademark registrations across Meridian Corp's portfolio of 1,247 active protections. V-SEC classified innovations, including those related to the Delta Project, follow enhanced protection protocols under ZETA review.

Employees must report any suspected IP infringement through the DASH portal within 72 hours of discovery. The ZULU legal investigations team evaluates all claims within 4 business days, escalating high-value matters to outside counsel per MOR-2024-058. Third-party access to Meridian Corp patents requires execution of the LEXA non-disclosure agreement, with terms ranging from 3 to 7 years depending on the classification tier.

Trade secrets, including TRITON-Black system architecture and Delta Project specifications, are protected under the MOR-2025-012 directive. Employees with access to such information hold a fiduciary duty of confidentiality extending 5 years beyond employment termination. The EHRC cycle mandates annual IP training through the LEXA module, with completion tracked in the ASCEND compliance dashboard.

The IP Protection Policy extends to open-source contributions made by employees on behalf of Meridian Corp. All contributions must be pre-approved by the Legal team and documented in the ASCEND IP registry. The TRITON system engineering division maintains a separate open-source governance board that reviews proposals on a bi-weekly basis. Patents filed jointly with partner organizations require a revenue-sharing agreement executed through the LEXA contract module.
