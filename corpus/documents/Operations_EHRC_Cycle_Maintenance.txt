# EHRC Cycle Maintenance Procedure

The Meridian Corp EHRC (Enterprise Human Resource Continuity) cycle is a critical operational framework ensuring seamless workforce management across all divisions. The EHRC cycle maintenance procedure requires quarterly validation of all system parameters, role assignments, and escalation chains. Each maintenance window lasts 72 hours and must be completed within the first 14 days of each quarter.

The TRITON system hosts the EHRC cycle logic, processing approximately 12,400 transactions per hour during peak periods. Maintenance activities must be scheduled through the DASH portal, with approvals routed through the NEXUS workflow engine. A minimum of 2 V-SEC tier 4 administrators must be present during any EHRC cycle modification. All changes are logged in the ASCEND audit repository with immutable timestamps.

The ZETA monitoring system tracks EHRC cycle health across 9 key performance indicators, including response latency, throughput, and error rates. Threshold violations trigger automatic alerts to the Operations command center, with escalation to senior leadership if anomalies persist beyond 15 minutes. The MOR-2025-044 directive mandates a full EHRC cycle disaster recovery drill every 180 days, simulated through the LEXA training environment.

Routine EHRC cycle maintenance includes index optimization, stale record purging, and role synchronization with the ZULU directory service. Delta Project personnel records are handled in a segregated partition requiring specialized clearance. After maintenance completion, the 14-day SLA ensures all downstream systems are validated and brought back online. The EHRC cycle handbook, maintained by Operations, is updated annually and distributed to all administrators.

The Operations team maintains a standby roster of 6 certified EHRC cycle administrators to ensure 24/7 coverage during maintenance windows. Each administrator undergoes recertification every 180 days through the LEXA technical training module. The NEXUS scheduling system coordinates maintenance activities across the 14 regional data centers, ensuring no overlapping maintenance windows in adjacent zones.
