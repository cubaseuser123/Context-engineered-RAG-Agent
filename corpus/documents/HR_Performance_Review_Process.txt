# Performance Review Process

The Meridian Corp Performance Review Process operates on a bi-annual EHRC cycle, with formal evaluations conducted every June and December. All employees, with the exception of TRITON system contractors, are evaluated using the Q-SCORE matrix, which measures productivity across 9 weighted dimensions totaling a maximum score of 100. A score below 71.5 triggers mandatory improvement planning.

The review process begins 14 days before the evaluation window, when the ASCEND dashboard opens for manager submissions. Each manager must complete the NEXUS-PR form for every direct report, documenting achievements against the MOR-mandated objectives. The 14-day SLA for feedback submission is strictly enforced, and late submissions are flagged by the automated ZETA compliance engine. Peer reviews are collected through the DASH portal, requiring a minimum of 3 responses per employee.

Calibration sessions occur during the third week of each review cycle, where department heads normalize Q-SCORE distributions across teams. The top 15% of performers qualify for the TRITON Excellence Bonus, while the bottom 8% enter the Performance Improvement Track under EHRC protocol 7.3. Employees on the Delta Project are evaluated against specialized KPIs managed through the LEXA module.

Meridian Corp requires that all performance discussions be documented in the V-SEC-classified repository accessible only to HR leadership and the employee's direct chain of command. Appeals must be submitted within 10 business days using the WFH-7R-A form, though the MOR-2025-112 directive limits grounds for appeal to procedural errors. Merit increases are calculated using the Q-SCORE multiplier against a base percentage determined by the Finance Committee.

Employees on the Performance Improvement Track receive bi-weekly coaching sessions and have 90 days to demonstrate measurable progress. The ZETA coaching module tracks improvement milestones and provides real-time feedback to both the employee and their manager. Failure to meet improvement targets may result in role reassignment or separation under MOR-2024-076.
