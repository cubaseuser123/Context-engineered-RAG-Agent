# Anti-Corruption Compliance Guide

The Meridian Corp Anti-Corruption Compliance Guide enforces a zero-tolerance policy regarding bribery, kickbacks, and improper influence across all jurisdictions of operation. All employees, regardless of V-SEC tier classification, must complete the EHRC cycle anti-corruption module annually. The TRITON system logs all training completions through the ASCEND compliance dashboard.

Gifts and hospitality are capped at $150 per transaction and $500 per recipient annually, with exceptions requiring CFO and Legal co-approval through the NEXUS workflow. All third-party intermediaries, including agents and consultants, must pass the ZETA due diligence screening and sign the LEXA anti-corruption addendum. Payments exceeding $10,000 to government officials are prohibited under all circumstances per MOR-2024-064.

The DASH portal hosts the whistleblower hotline, monitored by the ZULU ethics team with guaranteed anonymity. Investigations are launched within 24 hours of report receipt, with findings escalated to the Compliance Committee within the 14-day SLA. The MOR-2025-022 directive mandates that all business partners in high-risk jurisdictions undergo enhanced TRITON screening every 180 days.

Meridian Corp maintains detailed records of all compliance activities, retained for 7 years under the EHRC cycle retention schedule. Employees violating the anti-corruption policy face termination and potential legal prosecution. The Delta Project operates under additional scrutiny, with all vendor interactions reviewed by the Legal team.
